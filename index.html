Biography

Hi! I’m Anish Saxena, a PhD student at Georgia Tech. My interests lie at the intersection of computer architecture, systems, and security. I work with Prof. Moin Qureshi in the Memory Systems Lab.

My goal is to develop high-performance and secure systems and architectures. I have devised microarchitectural attacks like DABANGG and mitigations for hardware vulnerabilities like AQUA, improved the performance of next-generation cache and memory hierarchies, and enhanced simulation methodologies for ARM and x86 based architectures.

I have been fortunate to be mentored by my undergraduate advisor Prof. Biswabandan Panda (IIT Bombay) and Prof. Mahendra Verma (IIT Kanpur) as well as by industrial collaborators as part of my internships and projects at Intel Labs, NVIDIA, Qualcomm Research and Micron.
