Biography

Hi! I’m Anish Saxena, a senior undergraduate at IIT Kanpur. My interests lie broadly in computer systems.

I work with Biswa in CAR3S Group. We recently made public our work on improved side-channel attacks using flush-based timing techniques in processor caches. We named it the DABANGG Attack. This work was funded by NXP Semiconductors through SRC.

At CAR3S, we are formulating a framework to collect and analyze memory access traces from Android applications for ARM architecture. This work is sponsored by Qualcomm Research. I recently pursued a Research Intern in Processor Architecture Research Lab at Intel Labs. We improved performance of a non-inclusive cache hierarchy by identifying and tracking parameters such as reuse distance.

If you’re interested, I’ve found a Medium to present my experiences in words. I’m compiling a fairly comprehensive set of resources to introduce folks to computer systems, the details of which are available here: Systems Reading Group.
